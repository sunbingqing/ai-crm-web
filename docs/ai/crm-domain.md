# CRM Domain

## Core Objects

- Lead: an unqualified person or company with potential intent.
- Contact: a known person tied to an account.
- Account: a company, buying group, or strategic customer.
- Opportunity: a revenue event with stage, value, owner, and close plan.
- Activity: calls, emails, meetings, tasks, notes, and AI-generated follow-ups.
- Conversation: a call, email thread, chat, or meeting transcript with intent and sentiment.
- Playbook: a repeatable sales motion such as renewal rescue, competitor displacement, or expansion.
- Signal: a detected buying event, risk, objection, champion move, or next-best action.

## Workflows

- Triage inbound leads by fit, urgency, buying intent, and channel.
- Coach reps before and after calls with account context and objection guidance.
- Track pipeline health by stage, coverage, risk, aging, and next action.
- Recommend follow-ups based on transcript content and CRM history.
- Help managers inspect team risk and coaching opportunities.

## AI Behavior

- AI suggestions must be explainable. Show the evidence, freshness, and confidence.
- AI output should propose actions, not just summarize data.
- Keep human control visible for sending messages, changing stages, and updating records.

## UI Vocabulary

Use concrete CRM labels such as:

- Next best action
- Buying committee
- Intent signal
- Risk reason
- Champion activity
- Forecast change
- Follow-up due
- Call coaching
- Pipeline coverage
- Deal health
